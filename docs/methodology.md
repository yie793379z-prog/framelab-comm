# Methodology

FrameLab is designed to support introductory communication research workflows. It does not automate methodology away. Instead, it helps students organize texts, test coding schemas, and revise early judgments in a structured way.

## What Content Analysis Is

Content analysis is a systematic way of examining media or communication texts by applying categories to recurring features in the material.

In classroom settings, students often use content analysis to ask questions such as:

- Which themes appear most often?
- What tone is used in social media posts?
- Which actors are emphasized in coverage?
- What calls to action are present?

FrameLab supports this by letting users import text samples, apply a template, edit coding values, and export a structured result.

## What Framing Analysis Is

Framing analysis focuses on how an issue is presented, not only whether it is mentioned.

Typical framing questions include:

- What is defined as the problem?
- Who is seen as responsible?
- What moral or emotional lens is used?
- What solutions are suggested?

FrameLab’s `News Framing Analysis` template is a lightweight starting point for this kind of work. It is not a substitute for a full framing theory review, but it can help students begin identifying patterns in how issues are constructed.

## What Human-in-the-Loop Coding Means

Human-in-the-loop coding means the tool assists the researcher, but the researcher remains responsible for interpretation.

In FrameLab, that means:

- templates provide structured starting points
- local mock suggestions or optional real AI suggestions provide editable draft values
- users can and should revise every field
- exported outputs are records of a coding workflow, not automatic conclusions

This approach is especially important in communication research because many coding decisions depend on context, theory, and careful reading rather than surface keywords alone.

## Why AI Suggestions Should Be Reviewed and Edited

Even plausible suggestions can be analytically weak.

For example:

- a post may look promotional but function mainly as issue advocacy
- a crisis statement may contain an apology without truly accepting responsibility
- a quote may sound important but not actually represent the sample’s core meaning

That is why FrameLab treats AI assistance as a draft layer only. The user should always review, correct, and justify the final coding choices.

## How Students Can Use FrameLab

FrameLab is especially useful for:

- coursework exercises in news, media, or journalism classes
- pilot coding before a larger thesis project
- small framing or content-analysis assignments
- preparing early codebooks and sample summaries
- comparing English and Chinese communication materials in a single lightweight workspace

## Suggested Student Workflow

1. Start with a small sample set.
2. Choose the template closest to your research question.
3. Code a few items manually first.
4. Use AI suggestions only after you understand the template logic.
5. Revise fields where the tool is too generic or too confident.
6. Export results for class discussion, memo writing, or further analysis.
